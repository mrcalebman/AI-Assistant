## Pre-Construction

### 3.1 Handoff Meeting with Salesperson
- Review contract, scope, budget, timeline
- Discuss client expectations and background
- Identify key milestones
- Clarify communication protocol
- Verify materials and selections
- Review potential challenges
- Align on next steps
- Salesperson starts group message with PM and customer
- Document in "PM to Salesperson Handoff Checklist"

### 3.2 Requesting & Reviewing Sub Estimates
- On design-phase projects, salesperson has already gathered estimates — verify any remaining
- Get competitive estimates; exceptions for trusted regular subs
- Confirm sub availability before committing to schedule
- Send sub all trade-specific details
- Send "Subcontractor Onboarding Email" — collect W-9, COI, signed Sub Agreement
- Review SOW with each sub
- Create master schedule with sub timelines and milestones
- Brief all subs on trash management plan and jobsite expectations

### 3.3 Building Scope of Work in Trello
- Review estimate and before photos in Houzz before handoff meeting
- Note unclear areas or potential problems
- Document all tasks, phases, and deadlines in Trello
- Break into stages (demo, framing, mechanical rough-ins, drywall, finish carpentry, tile, flooring, cabinets, painting, plumbing/electric finishes, hardware, final inspection)
- Identify potential complications; include solutions
- List all materials and finishes
- Establish phase timelines and material ordering deadlines
- Share SOW with subs
- Regularly update SOW and design sheets throughout project
- Communicate SOW issues to salesperson before going to customer

### 3.4 Pulling Permits
- Confirm salesperson didn't already pull the permit
- Identify municipality; find correct application
- Call municipality to confirm permit is required
- Submit application with all required docs
- Keep records in project's Google Drive folder
- Plan for 2-week lead time between submission and receipt
- Schedule inspections as required
- Close out permits after final inspection
- Keep homeowner informed of permit/inspection status

### 3.5 Scheduling
- Establish start and finish dates with salesperson
- Build tentative schedule; confirm completion date is achievable
- Confirm sub and vendor availability; adjust as needed
- Standard phase sequence: Demo → Framing/Insulation → Mechanical Rough-ins → Rough-in Inspection → Drywall → Pre-paint Finish Carpentry → Tile → Flooring → Vanities/Cabinets/Countertops → Painting → Plumbing/Electric Finishes → Hardware → Final Inspection
- Watch for long lead times (countertops, custom cabinets, templating, etc.)
- Account for concurrent projects; avoid sub/team overlaps
- Communicate schedule changes to sales manager and client throughout

### 3.6 Ordering Materials
- Review selections with salesperson in handoff meeting; flag long lead items
- Create detailed material list (quantities, brands, types)
- Get supplier quotes; compare pricing and lead times
- Order early to account for delays or backorders
- Schedule "just in time" deliveries — avoid materials piling up on site
- Order small items to Redemption office for temporary storage
- Track delivery dates; inspect materials on arrival
- Always order 15% extra for shapable materials (flooring, tile, drywall, framing)
- For large Home Depot orders, run through Luke Beers or another sales rep for max discounts

### 3.7 Cost Management
- Review estimate; each line item cost = budget for that item
- Inspect each sub estimate — scope must match ours, price must fit budget
- Review actual material costs against estimate
- If something exceeds budget: find alternate source, propose to sales manager, evaluate solutions
- Check in with subs throughout to ensure they can stay on budget

### 3.7.1 Paying Subcontractors
- Payment issued within 2 weeks of completed work
- Verify completed work against scope and quality standards
- Collect: detailed invoice, W-9, COI, signed Sub Agreement
- Cross-check invoice against contracted amount
- Apply retainage if applicable
- Send invoice to finance@redemptionhr.com with: job address, trade category, work verified complete, completion date
- Confirm payment was processed; notify sub

## Construction Management

### 4.1 Initial Walk-through with Customer and Salesperson
- Salesperson introduces PM to customer
- Salesperson walks through SOW in chronological order with as much detail as possible
- Salesperson asks for questions throughout
- After walk-through, help set up barriers/protection
- Should be the very first thing done on day one
- Lasts 30–60 minutes; give customer advance notice

### 4.2 Jobsite Management
- Be kind — we are guests in their home
- Set up safety protocols (barriers, warning signs, PPE)
- Keep site organized, tools and materials in designated storage areas
- Inspect regularly for organization and cleanliness
- Minimize tools/materials on site; avoid customer disruption

#### 4.2.1 Customer Experience
- Greet customer daily; ask if anything is needed
- Notify customer when leaving (momentarily or end of day)
- Communicate weekly: when things will happen on site
- Communicate daily: what was accomplished, when you'll be back, what's next
- Protect flooring and surfaces; limit work area access
- Minimize noise/disruption; schedule loud tasks at reasonable hours
- Daily cleanup; respect privacy; no smoking/vaping on property
- Brief subs on customer experience expectations
- "Surprise and Delight" — find one small way to exceed expectations each day

#### 4.2.2 Maintaining a Clean Jobsite
- Daily cleaning routine; designated trash bins per section
- Assign team member to monitor end-of-day cleanliness
- Secure site at end of day
- Regular debris removal

#### 4.2.3 Trash Management
- Assess scope to determine trash volume per phase
- Determine best removal method (dumpster? weekly pickup?)
- Brief every sub on trash expectations BEFORE work begins
- If sub generates ≤1 bag of trash, they take it with them
- One trash can with bags stays on site throughout project
- Schedule weekly trash removal for sites without dumpsters
- No trash outside the work area

#### 4.2.4 Material Management
- "Just In Time" system — materials arrive when needed, not before
- Assess when each material is needed per phase
- Minimize materials stored on site at any one time

### 4.3 Communicating with Homeowners
- Set communication expectations from the start
- Provide updates at minimum every other day; daily for occupied houses
- Give 24-hour advance notice before contractors enter occupied homes
- Respond to client questions/concerns within 24 hours
- Alert to delays or issues immediately with transparent solutions
- Document all communications

#### 4.3.1 Change Orders
- Any scope change: discuss with salesperson before talking to customer
- Change orders must be initiated by salesperson and signed by customer before work is performed
- Document change in detail (scope, materials, labor)
- Get written approval before proceeding
- Assess impact on cost and timeline
- Update contract; communicate to all subs and team members
- Adjust project timeline accordingly
- Track change order costs

### 4.6.2 Final Walkthrough with Homeowners
- Schedule after all work and punch list items are complete
- Prepare punch list of outstanding items
- Walk through every room with homeowner
- Explain maintenance and warranty details
- Fix any remaining issues requested during walkthrough
- Review final payments; ensure contract is settled
- Provide all documents (warranties, inspection reports)
- Express gratitude; encourage referrals
- After final touchups approved, ask for Google review: https://g.page/r/CXVA7akwu3OSEBM/review

### Client Relations
- Group message with customer, PM, and salesperson for all communication
- Customers cannot give instructions directly to subcontractors — must go through PM or salesperson
- Respond to client questions within 24 hours
- Progress photos at minimum every other day; daily for occupied houses
- Strong client relationships → repeat business and referrals
- Trust is the currency between customers and PMs
