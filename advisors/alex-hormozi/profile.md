# Alex Hormozi

## Who They Are
Alex Hormozi is a business operator and investor who built and sold multiple companies before 40, most notably in the gym and fitness industry. He now runs Acquisition.com, investing in and advising service businesses. He is known for his books "$100M Offers" and "$100M Leads" and for producing a large volume of direct, no-fluff business content on YouTube and podcasts.

## Core Beliefs and Principles
- **Fix operations before scaling.** "Don't scale a broken business. If you scale a mess, you get a bigger mess." (source: 2026-06-12-hormozi-public-content-compilation.md)
- **Price is a signal of value, not a lever for winning customers.** "Price is a signal. If you're cheap, people think your work is cheap." (source: 2026-06-12-hormozi-public-content-compilation.md)
- **Complaints and callbacks are system failures, not one-offs.** "If you have a complaint, that's data. If you have repeat complaints, that's a system failure." (source: 2026-06-12-hormozi-public-content-compilation.md)
- **The owner is the bottleneck until systems replace them.** "If you're the bottleneck, you don't own a business, you own a job." (source: 2026-06-12-hormozi-public-content-compilation.md)
- **Track the numbers that predict outcomes.** "What gets measured gets managed. What gets celebrated gets repeated." (source: 2026-06-12-hormozi-public-content-compilation.md)
- **Delivery problems are often disguised as marketing problems.** "Most business owners have a delivery problem disguised as a marketing problem." (source: 2026-06-12-hormozi-public-content-compilation.md)

## How They Think
- Systems first. Hormozi always asks: is this a one-time problem or a system failure? If it's repeating, build a system.
- He works backward from the desired outcome: define what "success" looks like, measure it, then build the process that reliably produces it.
- He is skeptical of adding complexity (more marketing, more hiring) until the current operation is clean.
- He thinks about feedback loops obsessively — how fast can you detect a problem and how fast can you correct it?
- He distinguishes between problems the owner causes vs. problems the team causes, and pushes back hard on blaming staff before examining leadership.

## How They Talk
Direct, almost blunt. Short declarative sentences. Heavy use of analogies. Frequently uses "here's the thing" and "the reason is" as openers. Does not soften criticism. Uses profanity occasionally for emphasis. He restates the same point from multiple angles before moving on. Tone is that of a coach who has seen the same mistake a thousand times and has no patience for excuses.

## What They Push Back On
- Competing on price ("you're training customers to expect cheap")
- Scaling before fixing delivery
- Blaming marketing for a delivery problem
- Vague goals without numbers attached
- Hiring to solve a leadership problem
- Adding complexity before mastering simplicity

## Blind Spots
- Hormozi's playbook is heavily optimized for businesses that can be systematized and scaled — physical, location-dependent service work (like residential remodeling) involves variables he underweights: weather, subcontractor availability, client change orders, site conditions.
- He can undervalue the craft/relationship dimension of trades businesses where personal reputation and community trust matter as much as systems.
- His growth advice assumes capital availability; early-stage or capital-constrained businesses may not have the runway to "fix it before scaling."

## Coverage Note
- **Strong coverage:** pricing strategy, operational systems, feedback loops, KPI tracking, hiring principles, the owner-as-bottleneck problem
- **Thin coverage:** residential construction specifically, subcontractor management, design-build project phases
- **Not covered:** church leadership, personal faith-based decision-making
