On pricing and value:
"You want to make your offer so good that people feel stupid saying no." The goal is not to be the cheapest — it is to make the value so undeniable that price becomes secondary.

"Price is a signal. If you're cheap, people think your work is cheap."

On operational systems before growth:
"Don't scale a broken business. If you scale a mess, you get a bigger mess." Hormozi is insistent that you must fix the operations first — before spending on marketing or adding customers.

"Most business owners have a delivery problem disguised as a marketing problem."

On quality and reputation:
"The best marketing is a good product." Long-form: if your product or service genuinely delivers results, word of mouth does the heavy lifting. Refunds, complaints, and callbacks are signals of a broken delivery system.

On hiring and team:
"A players hire A players. B players hire C players." The quality of your first hires sets the ceiling for everything you build.

"You cannot delegate your way out of a leadership problem." If the team isn't performing, the question is what the owner is or isn't doing, not just what the employee is or isn't doing.

On metrics and feedback loops:
"What gets measured gets managed. What gets celebrated gets repeated." Track the numbers that actually predict outcomes, not vanity metrics.

"If you have a complaint, that's data. If you have repeat complaints, that's a system failure."

On the business of contracting and service businesses:
"Service businesses are high margin if you control quality and don't compete on price." The trap most service businesses fall into is racing to the bottom on price. The solution is building a reputation that commands premium pricing.

On working on vs. in the business:
"If you're the bottleneck, you don't own a business, you own a job." The goal is to build systems and people so the business runs without the owner doing every job.

On growth timeline expectations:
"Everyone overestimates what they can do in a year and underestimates what they can do in ten." Patience combined with consistent execution beats urgency without discipline.

On feedback and iteration:
"Speed of implementation is a competitive advantage." Don't wait until you have a perfect plan — execute, measure, adjust.
