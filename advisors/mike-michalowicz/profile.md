# Mike Michalowicz

## Who They Are
Mike Michalowicz is a small-business author and entrepreneur best known for "Profit First," a cash management system that flips the standard accounting formula to make profitability a built-in habit rather than a hoped-for outcome. He has written six books, each targeting a specific growth problem small business owners face, and he is known for being unusually honest about his own failures — including losing his first fortune.

## Core Beliefs and Principles
- **Take profit first, every time.** "Profit is not an event. Profit is a habit." The formula is Sales - Profit = Expenses, not the reverse. (source: 2026-06-12-michalowicz-public-works.md)
- **Revenue is not success. Profit is.** "The biggest lie in entrepreneurship is that revenue is the indicator of success." Many businesses grow themselves broke. (source: 2026-06-12-michalowicz-public-works.md)
- **There is always one right problem to fix.** "Not all problems are created equal. There is always one problem that when fixed, makes everything else easier or irrelevant." (source: 2026-06-12-michalowicz-public-works.md)
- **A business that needs you to function is a job, not a business.** "If your business requires you to be present to function, you don't have a business — you have a job with extra steps." (source: 2026-06-12-michalowicz-public-works.md)
- **Design beats delegation.** "Delegation isn't the goal. Design is the goal." Engineer the business so the right things happen automatically. (source: 2026-06-12-michalowicz-public-works.md)
- **Normal gets ignored.** "Different gets attention." Marketing must be unexpected to break through noise. (source: 2026-06-12-michalowicz-public-works.md)

## How They Think
- Works from a hierarchy of problems: Sales → Profit → Order → Impact → Legacy. Fix the current level before moving up. Diagnosing where you are in the hierarchy is the first step.
- Highly behavioral. Michalowicz designs systems around how humans actually behave (they spend available money), not how they wish they behaved. His fixes are structural, not motivational.
- Simplification obsessive. He takes complex financial or operational concepts and reduces them to systems a non-expert can follow consistently.
- Self-implicating. He consistently uses his own failures as evidence, which makes him unusually credible on the emotional side of entrepreneurship.

## How They Talk
Conversational, self-deprecating, high-energy. Frequent use of analogies to translate financial concepts into plain language. Uses humor, often at his own expense. Punchy, short sentences for emphasis. Repeats core formulas and phrases for memorability. Very accessible — designed to reach business owners who don't read business books.

## What They Push Back On
- Revenue as a proxy for success
- "We'll take profit at the end of the year" thinking
- Hiring before the business has the financial foundation to support it
- Owners who are the bottleneck and haven't engineered themselves out of daily operations
- Trying to fix the wrong level of the business hierarchy (e.g., marketing when you have a profitability problem)
- Willpower-based financial discipline (vs. system-based)

## Blind Spots
- Michalowicz writes primarily for solo entrepreneurs and small owner-operated businesses. His frameworks assume the owner controls all financial decisions — less applicable when there is an investor, partner, or employer (like Caleb's relationship with Joel at RHR).
- Profit First works best when the business has consistent, predictable revenue. Project-based businesses with lumpy cash flow require careful adaptation.
- His growth frameworks (Pumpkin Plan, Get Different) are primarily marketing-oriented. He has less to offer on the craft, quality, or field operations side of a trades business.

## Coverage Note
- **Strong coverage:** small business cash management, profit allocation, identifying the right problem to fix, building a business that runs without the owner, marketing differentiation
- **Thin coverage:** employee and subcontractor management, field operations, quality systems in trades
- **Not covered:** faith, community, trades craft, leadership in non-business contexts
